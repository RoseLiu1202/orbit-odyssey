Orbital Odyssey Process Book

Background and Motivation

Our project aims to create a web-based 3D experience that brings to life the rich narrative of space exploration over the past 2 years. Using Three.js and WebXR, we're crafting an accessible, immersive visualization that invites users to interactively explore space missions, highlighting the immense achievements and challenges faced by those who reach beyond our planet. Through engaging 3D graphics and VR-ready features, users will journey through key moments in space exploration history, witnessing the trials, failures, and triumphs that have driven humankind's quest to understand and expand our boundaries.

Objectives

Key Questions

  • What milestones have we made since the first human landing on moon in 1969?
  • How is space exploration a global effort and what are some key countries and agencies?
  • How far has human reached the universe and what exciting frontiers are next on our journey?
  • What are different types of progress we have made, including technology advancement, distance traveled, etc?

Benefits

  • Enhanced Public Understanding: Viewers gain a deeper knowledge of space exploration and the technology behind it, bridging complex topics with clear visuals.
  • Increased Interest in Space Science: Inspiring curiosity in space-related fields can attract more interest and support for space programs.
  • Bridge Space Knowledge with Everyday Life: For instance, showing how certain satellite discoveries impact technology, climate science, or global communication.

Technology Stack

  • Three.js: Core 3D graphics engine for creating interactive space visualizations
  • WebXR: Optional immersive VR experience support
  • JavaScript/HTML/CSS: Core web technologies for the application structure
  • React: User interface development
  • D3.js: Additional data visualization components
  • WebGL: 3D rendering capabilities

Data

Our primary data source is the Timeline of Space Exploration from Wikipedia, which was carefully curated and categorized to suit our visualization needs:

Timeline of Space Exploration - Wikipedia

Data processing involved manual curation, categorization of events by type, standardization of data formats, and thorough verification for accuracy. We focused on significant space exploration milestones and achievements to create a comprehensive visualization of humanity's journey into space.

Must Have Features

  • Major Space Events Timeline
    • Annual timeline of significant space events
    • Click interaction for users to select specific years
    • Interactive window for filtering space events by type (Technological Achievement, Human Spaceflight, Planetary Exploration)
  • Interactive Timeline
    • Use a slider to let users scroll through time
    • Highlight missions that occurred during the selected period
    • Filter options for mission types, countries, and exploration targets
  • Launch Location and Date Visualization
    • Clearly display each object's launch site and date
    • Provide spatial and temporal context to help users track the journey from Earth to space
    • Visualize targeted planetary bodies for exploration missions
  • Interactive Info Panels
    • Each object includes info panels detailing scientific data, mission objectives, and relevant imagery
    • These panels enhance the educational aspect, offering users an in-depth understanding of the purpose and achievements of each mission
    • Display country of origin, mission name, and specific achievements

Optional Features

  • Real-Time Orbit Display: Visualize satellites and other objects in their current orbits around Earth, providing users with an authentic sense of their position and movement in space.
  • Astronaut's Perspective Simulation: Allow users to "float" in space around key objects like the ISS or specific satellites, creating an immersive experience that simulates an astronaut's view and proximity to these technologies.

Implementation

In the final implementation, we combined interactivity, storytelling, and visual aesthetics to create an engaging user experience. The following components were implemented:

  • Interactive Timeline: A vertical bar chart powered by Chart.js allows users to explore the progression of space exploration milestones from 1969 onwards. Each bar represents the number of milestones achieved in a given year, and the bars are color-coded by country (e.g., USA in blue, USSR in red). Users can filter data using an intuitive dropdown menu for country selection.
  • 3D Solar System Visualization: We used Three.js to create an interactive 3D solar system. As users interact with the timeline or explore specific years, planets gradually light up, visually representing humanity's expansion into the solar system.
  • Dynamic Milestone Cards: For every milestone, a detailed card appears in the center of the screen, providing mission-specific information such as mission name, technology advancement, and discoveries. Users can navigate between milestones using forward and backward controls.

Evaluation

Insights from the Data

Using the visualizations, we uncovered several insights about the progression of space exploration:

  • There was a clear surge in space missions in the 1970s, primarily driven by the USA and USSR.
  • The number of achievements declined in the late 1980s and early 1990s, but there was a resurgence in the 2000s with the contributions of new players like China and private companies.
  • Modern milestones often focus on interplanetary exploration, deep space telescopes, and international collaborations like the ISS.

Effectiveness of the Visualization

Our visualization effectively addresses the key questions:

  • Notable Achievements: The milestones and interactive cards highlight key missions across decades.
  • Global Effort: Color-coded visualizations clearly showcase contributions from various countries.
  • Expansion into Space: The 3D solar system vividly represents the progression of humanity's exploration beyond Earth.

Limitations and Future Improvements

  • While the 3D solar system is visually impressive, the lack of trajectory animations limits the dynamic storytelling potential.
  • We could improve the interactivity of the solar system by allowing users to zoom into specific planets and view related missions.
  • Including real-time updates on ongoing missions (e.g., NASA or SpaceX data feeds) would enhance the educational value of the visualization.

Overall, the final visualization successfully balances aesthetics, interactivity, and storytelling to engage users and promote a deeper understanding of space exploration.
